Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about StudyTool's features.

General & Account

StudyTool is an all-in-one platform designed to help students succeed. It allows you to analyze your academic performance, create study schedules, test your knowledge with quizzes, and connect with other students.

You can view your current information on your Profile page. The "Edit Profile" functionality is currently under development and will be available soon.

Performance Analysis

The analysis tool allows you to manually enter your scores from two different tests. You provide the test name, the marks you obtained, and the total marks for the exam. The system then calculates your performance as a percentage for each test and displays a bar chart to help you compare your results.

Schedule

On the Schedule page, you can use the form at the top to add a new event. Simply enter a title, choose the event type (Study Session or Exam), and select the date and time. Your new event will then appear on the calendar below.

Yes, absolutely. Your schedule is completely private and only you can see the events you create.

Study Tips & Motivation

Fear often comes from feeling overwhelmed. The best way to combat this is to break the subject down into very small, manageable parts. Start with the most basic concept, even if it feels too simple, and don't move on until you understand it. Celebrate small wins! Mastering one small topic builds the confidence you need to tackle the next one.

Technical subjects are best learned by doing, not just reading. Follow these steps:
  • Understand the Concept: First, focus on understanding the core theory or formula.
  • Practice Problems: Immediately apply the concept by solving problems. Start with easy ones and gradually increase the difficulty.
  • Teach It: Try to explain the concept to someone else (or even to yourself). This is called the Feynman Technique and is incredibly effective at revealing gaps in your understanding.

Procrastination often happens when a task seems too big. Try the Pomodoro Technique: set a timer for 25 minutes and focus completely on one task. When the timer rings, take a 5-minute break. After four sessions, take a longer break. This makes starting feel less daunting. Also, set clear, achievable goals for each study session to build momentum.

Both methods have their strengths!
- Studying alone is essential for deep focus and memorizing core material.
- Studying in a group is excellent for discussing complex topics, getting different perspectives, and solving difficult problems. You can use the Group Chat feature on our site to connect with classmates, ask questions, and collaborate. A combination of both is often the most effective strategy.
